Teaching

I believe that everyone can have meaningful and empowering mathematical learning experiences. My teaching philosophy centers on clarity, intuition, and helping students build confidence in their ability to reason through complex ideas. Over the past ten-plus years, I have worked extensively as an individual tutor, supporting students with diverse backgrounds, goals, and learning styles. As a teaching assistant, I aim to create an inclusive and supportive classroom environment that emphasizes conceptual understanding alongside technical skill. I am particularly interested in helping students connect formal theory with practical application. A more detailed discussion of my approach can be found in my teaching statement.

Selected Student Profiles

Undergraduate pre-med student struggling with physics

Worked with a pre-med undergraduate enrolled in calculus-based physics who was struggling to connect mathematical formalism with physical intuition. Through targeted problem-solving strategies and conceptual explanations, the student improved exam performance and gained confidence applying physics principles independently.

High school Algebra I student with a busy schedule

Supported a high school student balancing athletics and extracurricular commitments while taking Algebra I. Sessions focused on efficient study strategies, foundational skill-building, and exam preparation, resulting in improved grades and reduced math-related anxiety.

Middle school student getting ahead in math

Tutored a motivated middle school student seeking enrichment beyond the standard curriculum. We explored advanced problem-solving techniques and previewed algebraic concepts, helping the student develop strong mathematical intuition and enthusiasm for the subject.
