Research

My research lies at the intersection of probability, combinatorics, and stochastic processes. I am particularly interested in mixing times of Markov chains and card shuffling models. My work focuses on probabilistic applications of algebraic tools from representation theory and symmetric function theory to better understand convergence phenomena.

  2. Tensor powers of virtual symmetric group representations encoding permutation cycle type statistics, FPSAC 2026 Extended Abstract Submission, 2025.

    Abstract

    It is well known that the character of the defining representation of \(S_n\) over \(\mathbb{C}\), \(\chi^\varrho(\sigma)\) encodes the number of fixed points of a permutation \(\sigma \in S_n\). It is hard in general to take tensor products of representations of \(S_n\), but it is known how to decompose tensor powers of the defining representation as a direct sum of irreducible representations. Alon and Kozma gave a decomposition of the \(S_n\)-class function \( a_j(\sigma)=\{\text{# of \(j\)-cycles of \(\sigma\)}\}\) in terms of irreducible characters. This class function is the character of a virtual representation of \(S_n\). We describe how to decompose tensor powers of this virtual representation as a linear combination of irreducible representations when \(n\) is large enough. As an application, we state a new result concerning \(j\)-cycle mixing via the random \(i\)-cycle walk on \(S_n\).
