Research

My research lies at the intersection of probability, combinatorics, and stochastic processes. I am particularly interested in mixing times of Markov chains and card shuffling models. My work focuses on probabilistic applications of algebraic tools from representation theory and symmetric function theory to better understand convergence phenomena.

Preprints & Publications

  1. Representation theory and cycle statistics for random walks on the symmetric group , Submitted to Electronic Journal of Combinatorics, 2026.

    Abstract

    We use representation theory of \(S_n\) to analyze the mixing of permutation cycle type statistics \( a_j(\sigma)=\{\text{# of \(j\)-cycles of \(\sigma\)}\}\) for any fixed \(j\) and \(\sigma\) resulting from a random \(i\)-cycle walk on \(S_n\). We also derive analogous results for the random star transposition walk. Our approach uses the method of moments; a key ingredient is a new formula for the coefficients in the irreducible character decomposition of the \(S_n\)-class function \((a_j)^r(\sigma)=\{(\text{# of \(j\)-cycles of \(\sigma\)})^r\}\) for any positive integers \(r,j\) when \(n \ge 2rj\).

  2. Tensor powers of virtual symmetric group representations encoding permutation cycle type statistics , FPSAC 2026 Extended Abstract Submission.

    Abstract

    It is well known that the character of the defining representation of \(S_n\) over \(\mathbb{C}\), \(\chi^\varrho(\sigma)\) encodes the number of fixed points of a permutation \(\sigma \in S_n\). We describe how to decompose tensor powers of a virtual representation encoding cycle statistics as a linear combination of irreducible representations when \(n\) is sufficiently large. As an application, we obtain new results concerning \(j\)-cycle mixing via the random \(i\)-cycle walk on \(S_n\).
